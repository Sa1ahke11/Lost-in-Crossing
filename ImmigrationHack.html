In 2016 alone, 322 people died crossing the US southwest border.

215 of those died in Texas.

Hundreds of bodies found in the desert have not been identified or returned to their families.

Volunteers throughout Texas are helping families find information about unidentified remains and navigate the long process to bring their loved ones home for burial.

About Lost in Crossing

A guide to finding, identifying, and bringing home the remains of migrants who die on the border

Lost is Crossing is a resource to help people find loved ones who have gone missing or died while crossing the southwestern border of the United States, and guide them through the process of retrieving their remains. This website is maintained and updated by volunteers.

If you need help to find a missing loved one, see the Resources page. Organizations like South Texas Human Rights Center are ready to guide you.

Yo Tengo Nombre/I Have a Name

Yo Tengo Nombre/I Have a Name shares all available information about unidentified migrants who died crossing through Brooks County, Texas.

If you believe you recognize someone on Yo Tengo Nombre/I Have a Name, go to the Resources page to begin the process of retrieving their remains.

If you do not see your loved one’s information in Yo Tengo Nombre/I Have a Name and need help locating them, contact search groups and law enforcement on the Resources page.
